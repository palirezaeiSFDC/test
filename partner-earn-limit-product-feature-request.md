# Product Feature Request

## Request Title
Partner Earn Limit Framework with Linkage-Aware Enforcement and Auditability

## Requestor Information
- Team: Salesforce Professional Services Team
- Contact:
  - Pegah Alirezaei, palirezaei@salesforce.com
  - Tamir Weinberger, tweinberger@salesforce.com
- Date Submitted: June 1, 2026

## High-Level Problem Statement
Marriott needs a controlled and scalable Partner Earn model that enforces earning limits while supporting multi-partner linkage scenarios (for example, Amex and Uber), partner-specific liability handling, and complete transaction-level auditability.

Today, partner earning behavior spans both partner-precalculated points and Salesforce-calculated points, with varying constraints by partner, product, and bonus code. Without a unified and configurable limits framework, there is risk of over-crediting, inconsistent enforcement, unclear ownership of liability, and operational complexity as partner volume grows.

Requested outcome: a productized capability to configure and enforce Global Partner Limits, Individual Partner Limits, and Product/Bonus-level limits with clear transaction outcomes (credit vs non-credit), auditable reason codes, and alignment to existing linkage and DPE liability processes.

## Business Need and Justification
- Protect financial exposure by enforcing configurable partner earn caps.
- Support growth to ~180-200 partners and very high member linkage volume.
- Ensure consistent enforcement regardless of whether points are partner-precalculated or Salesforce-calculated.
- Preserve compliance and auditability by logging non-crediting outcomes and reasons in transaction records.
- Align earning liability outcomes to existing DPE postpaid/prepaid calculations.
- Enable future partner onboarding without repeated custom implementation.

## Use Cases / User Stories
- As a loyalty member linked to one or more partners, I should earn points only within configured limits and applicable linkage rules.
- As an operations admin, I need configurable limits by timeframe (days/weeks/months/years) at global, partner, and product/bonus levels.
- As finance, I need clarity on when liabilities remain with partner vs Marriott and complete reporting on non-credited points due to limits.
- As integration teams, I need deterministic behavior when partner transactions arrive independently for each partner.
- As support, I need traceable reason codes when a transaction is not credited due to limit enforcement.

## Proposed Solution (High-Level) and Current State
### Proposed capability
- Configure limits at three levels:
  - Global Partner Limit
  - Individual Partner Limit (including per-transaction controls where required)
  - Individual Product/Bonus Code Limit
- Support configurable limit timeframe units: Days, Weeks, Months, Years.
- Track earned-to-date values to evaluate limits at transaction time.
- Prevent crediting when limits are exceeded and stamp non-credit reason for audit.
- Keep Transaction Journal to Partner relationship as 1:1; separate partner transactions are processed separately (for example, Amex transaction in its own TJ, Uber in its own TJ).

### Current state and dependency behavior
- TIP currently calls Salesforce for link and delink events only.
- Link creates Partner-Member Linkage record with ACID, PartnerId, and PartnerAccountNumber.
- Delink identifies linkage via PartnerAccountNumber and deactivates/deletes linkage.
- Internal tier assessment logic runs on both link and delink events.
- Liability logic exists in DPE and must include incremental points generated by partner earning scenarios.

## Affected Systems and Integrations
- Salesforce Loyalty Management (core limits, rules, transaction processing).
- TIP integration for member-partner link/delink events.
- Partner systems (Amex, Uber, others) that provide transactions, spend/miles, or precomputed points.
- DPE job/process for liability calculation (postpaid/prepaid scenarios).
- Downstream financial reconciliation and reporting systems.

## Related Initiatives / Epics
- Partner earning enablement and limits control roadmap.
- Linkage and member tier uplift workflows tied to partner programs.
- Future promotions discovery for linked vs non-linked member behavior.

## Target Outcome
A supported and scalable implementation where:
- Partner earn limits are configurable and enforced consistently across global, partner, and product/bonus levels.
- Multiple partner transactions for the same member are handled independently and correctly.
- Non-crediting outcomes are fully auditable with reason tracking.
- Liability assignment remains aligned with DPE rules and scenario-specific ownership.
- Integration contracts support high-volume link/unlink and profile lookup patterns.

## Metrics for Success
- 100% of partner earn transactions evaluated against applicable limit hierarchy.
- 0 unauthorized credits beyond configured limits in production.
- Full traceability for non-credited transactions (reason captured in TJ/reporting).
- No material performance regression under expected high-volume processing.
- Faster partner onboarding due to reusable configuration model.

## Priority and Urgency
High Priority / Urgent.

Why urgent:
- Financial control risk increases with partner and linkage scale.
- Operational risk rises without standardized limit and audit enforcement.
- Delivery sequencing impacts upcoming partner onboarding and promotions planning.

## Functional Requirements
### 1) Limit Configuration Model
- Configure Global Partner Limit at program/member context.
- Configure Individual Partner Limit by partner and timeframe.
- Configure Individual Product/Bonus Code Limit and optional per-transaction ceilings.
- Support timeframe units: day/week/month/year.
- Define clear precedence/evaluation order across limits.

### 2) Linkage-Aware Eligibility
- Enforce earning only when member-partner linkage is active.
- Process one Transaction Journal per partner transaction (1:1 TJ-to-Partner relationship).
- Support two known linkage patterns:
  - Member linked to partner.
  - Member linked to partner category/product construct.

### 3) Transaction Evaluation and Crediting Behavior
- Accept both:
  - Partner-precalculated points, and
  - Salesforce-calculated points from spend/miles payloads.
- Evaluate limits before credit.
- If exceeded, do not credit points and store explicit non-credit reason.
- Ensure idempotent handling for retries/replays.

### 4) Liability and Ownership
- Incorporate additional points outcomes into DPE liability logic.
- Support scenario-based ownership (partner-funded vs Marriott-funded promotional contexts).
- Keep promotional liability decisioning extensible pending deeper product analysis.

### 5) Integration Contracts
- Link member API/input requirements: ACID, PartnerId, PartnerAccountNumber (plus extensible fields).
- Delink behavior based on unique partner account reference on linkage record.
- Get Member Partner Linkage support in high-volume profile retrieval patterns.
- Run integration flows under Integration User context.

### 6) Tracking and Reporting
- Track linked-member additional points and non-credit outcomes.
- Define source identifiers for additional points in ledger/reporting layers.
- Provide operational reports for limit breaches, non-credit reasons, linkage status, and partner trends.

### 7) Data and Field Alignment
- Validate and align required fields captured in discovery, including:
  - Partner Name, Member, Partner Member Account ID (ExternalIdentifier), Link Date, IsActive, Delink Date, and relevant optional metadata.
- Maintain extensibility for optional future fields (product, geography, referral, partner elite level, etc.).

## Non-Functional Requirements
- Scale: designed for high partner counts and very high linkage/transaction volumes.
- Performance: low-latency limit checks and transaction decisions under burst loads.
- Reliability: idempotent processing and robust retry behavior.
- Security: integration-user execution context and least-privilege access to configuration.
- Auditability: immutable transaction outcomes and reason-code traceability.
- Operability: monitorable metrics, alerting, and reconciliation support.
